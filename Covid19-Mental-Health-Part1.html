Mental Health Part 1

As a person living through 2020’s pandemic, many people are feeling the effects of the pandemic. There is one effect not many people are talking about as much. That effect is that during Covid19 mental illness is on the rise. A study from the CDC (that you can find here), the United States’ premier health and disease prevention government body has found that during COVID19, there has been a significant rise in various mental health problems. According to the CDC’s own report between mental illness and substance abuse 40% of American adults living in the United States are suffering from these ailments. This study was conducted in the end of June and was of adults 18 years and older. These ailments include but are not limited to anxiety, trauma-and stressor related disorder and suicidal thoughts. Some of the demographics that are extremely affected are various groups of minorities, caregivers, people that have been deemed as essential workers and younger adult. This is a crisis inside another crisis. We need to fight this crisis as much as the Covid19 crisis.
